CS101: Introduction to Programming & Computational Thinking

School of Computer Science & AI • 4.0 Credits • Fall Semester

Course Description & Objectives

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to computer science and the art of programming. We use **Python** as the primary teaching language, and introduce **C++** for performance analysis. Students will learn abstraction, algorithm design, fundamental data structures, and software engineering principles. The core goal is for students to analyze complex problems, decompose them into computational steps, and understand **Time and Space Complexity**.

Required Tools & Resources

PyCharm Pro (IDE) CLion (C++ IDE) GitHub Enterprise Linux/WSL Environment

Evaluation Criteria

Assignments & Labs (40%) / Midterm Project (20%) / Final Capstone (25%) / Participation (15%)
